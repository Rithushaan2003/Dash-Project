# Step 8 – Mean Opinion Score (MOS) Evaluation (ITU‑T BT.500)
The Mean Opinion Score (MOS) is a subjective video‑quality rating method defined in ITU‑T BT.500.
The same video clip was played under each network condition from Steps 6 and 7, and the perceived quality was rated using the standard 1–5 MOS scale:

• 	5 – Excellent (no visible impairments)

• 	4 – Good (slight impairments, not annoying)

• 	3 – Fair (noticeable but acceptable impairments)

• 	2 – Poor (annoying impairments)

• 	1 – Bad (very annoying / unwatchable)

The MOS score was assigned based on observed smoothness, freezing, buffering, and visual artifacts.

---

## MOS Results
| Scenario | Observed Behaviour | MOS |
|---------|--------------------|-----|
| Step 4 – DASH Adaptive Streaming | Smooth playback, minimal stutter | 4–5 |
| Step 5 – Non‑Adaptive Streaming | Freezing and stuttering; timeline continued while video paused | 2–3 |

---

Conclusion
• 	Adaptive streaming (Step 4) maintained high video quality even under varying network conditions.
• 	Non‑adaptive streaming (Step 5) suffered from freezing and timeline desynchronisation, resulting in lower MOS.
• 	These results demonstrate the advantage of adaptive bitrate streaming for maintaining user‑perceived quality.
